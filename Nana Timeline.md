# Umakant Bhargava — Life Timeline
### November 3, 1936 – April 28, 2021

---

## ✦ 1936 — Born in India
**November 3rd, 1936**

Umakant Bhargava was born on a November morning, beginning a life of curiosity, engineering brilliance, and warmth that would span 84 years.

---

## ◎ Childhood — Sports & Early Life
**Home & Neighbourhood**

Grew up playing hockey and cricket in the streets, often using fruit as a makeshift ball. Showed a love for games and camaraderie that stayed with him throughout life.

---

## ◈ ~1955 — Maharaja College, Jaipur
**Jaipur, Rajasthan**

Studied at Maharaja College, home to over 4,000 students. Played volleyball with such ferocity that "if it hit someone on the head, they'd be done for." Loved his mathematics professor who made calculus classes ring with laughter — posing questions to students and letting everyone learn together.

---

## ◈ 1958–59 — Engineering at BITS Pilani
**Pilani, Rajasthan**

Joined the famed Birla Institute of Technology & Science. Lived in a 144-person hostel, ate from pressed steel thalis cooked on a 4-foot tawa, and played volleyball, hockey, and carrom. Witnessed Major Dhyan Chand visit to teach hockey. Famously blew air into mango pits, stacked 101 of them on a table, and watched hostel friends scramble excitedly — only to find them hollow.

---

## ✦ 1959 — Engineering Degree Completed
**BITS Pilani**

Graduated with his engineering degree — just before the India-China War of 1962. A milestone that set the stage for a remarkable career in India's industrial development.

---

## ⬡ 1959 onwards — Training at Central Research Institute
**Pilani, Rajasthan**

Trained at the newly opened Central Research Institute in Pilani, specialising in electronics and mechanical operations. Spent time on lathes, milling machines, and drill presses — mastering hands-on engineering craft.

---

## ⬡ Early 1960s — Joined BHEL, Bhopal
**Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh**

Joined Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited (BHEL) — India's premier public sector engineering company. Lived in the BHEL hostel for two years before being allotted his first home. BHEL manufactured transformers, turbines, and switchgear using British and Siemens technology.

---

## ⬡ Various — Hyderabad Assignment
**Hyderabad**

Worked on a cathodic protection project for a sea refinery pipeline — safeguarding the pipe running from the jetty to the refinery. Even took a boat nearly a mile out to the jetty to supervise the work firsthand.

---

## ⬡ Various — Ankleshwar Assignment
**Ankleshwar, Gujarat**

Deployed for pipeline cathodic protection work, protecting oil pipelines stretching from Gujarat toward Uttar Pradesh — crucial infrastructure for India's growing energy network.

---

## ⬡ Various — Hazaribagh Pipeline Project
**Hazaribagh, Jharkhand**

Led cathodic protection on the Hazaribagh oil pipeline, preventing metal erosion caused by underground electrical currents using anodes to protect the steel pipes from corrosion.

---

## ⬡ Various — UP Traction Control Project
**Uttar Pradesh (6 months)**

Spent six months in UP working on traction control systems powering Bombay's suburban trains — overseeing high-voltage precision control systems that required expert handling.

---

## ⬡ Bachelor Years — Pune Posting
**Pune, Maharashtra**

Lived as a bachelor in a modest 10×12 ft room. By his own cheerful admission, it was swept only once in six months. Attended an Arjun Battle Tank seminar at Khadakwasla (NDA), asking sharper technical questions than the official delegates.

---

## ◉ 1980s — Arjun Battle Tank, Coordinator
**BHEL Bhopal / CVRDE**

His most distinguished project: coordinating the full assembly of India's indigenous Arjun Battle Tank prototype. He managed approximately 10,000 drawings, 7,000–8,000 parts, and 100 sub-assemblies. He identified critical design flaws — loose bolts vulnerable to vibration, missing locking mechanisms on tank tracks, and steel tolerances far too thick — and helped shape a stronger final design. Two prototype tanks were assembled under his coordination.

---

## ✦ Post-Retirement — Retirement from BHEL
**Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh**

After a distinguished career spanning cathodic protection, traction systems, and defence engineering, Nana retired from BHEL. His sharp technical mind never rested — he continued to analyse, discuss, and share wisdom with family until his final years.

---

## ✦ April 28, 2021 — Passed Away
**Age 84**

Umakant Bhargava left this world on April 28th, 2021. A life of 84 years filled with laughter at hostel pranks, precision in engineering, and love for family. His stories of laddoos the size of cricket balls, mango pit tricks, 50-roti dinners, and tank assembly drawings live on in those who loved him.

---

*"Whenever we eat good food, we remember it for the rest of our lives."*
*— Nana, as recalled by his family*
